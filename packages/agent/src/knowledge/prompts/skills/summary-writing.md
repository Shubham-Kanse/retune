# Professional Summary Writing

## Critical Context
Read the candidate's background before writing the summary. It must reflect their real voice, real experience, and real achievements — not generic filler. The summary must also be calibrated to the **specific company and role** using the company intel brief. A summary written for a fintech role should not read the same as one written for a legal-tech role, even for the same candidate.

---

## Formula (4–5 lines, 80–120 words)

```
Line 1: [Seniority] [Role] with [X years] experience in [industry/function]
Line 2: [Specific quantified achievement matching what THIS company cares about most]
Line 3: Top 4–5 skills pulled verbatim from the job description
Line 4: Unique differentiator — domain expertise, notable employer, or credential
Line 5 (optional): Cultural fit signal echoing the company's stated values
```

**ATS weight:** The first 50 words carry 4× the keyword weight of later text. Lead with the exact job title from the JD and the most critical keyword.

## Adapting for Any Role or Industry

If the target industry is not listed above:
1. Read the JD's stated responsibilities — the summary must echo those exact terms
2. Check the `company-intel` brief's "Resume Calibration Notes" for tone and emphasis
3. Use the formula: open with role/years, follow with the candidate's most relevant achievement, list JD's top keywords, close with differentiator

---

## Banned Phrases (instant AI-detection flag)

- "Passionate professional with a proven track record..."
- "Results-driven," "hardworking," "team player" without proof
- "Dynamic," "motivated," "enthusiastic," "exceptional," "outstanding"
- "Proven track record" in any form
- "Dedicated to delivering excellence"
- "A highly motivated self-starter"
- Any sentence that could belong to a different candidate unchanged

---

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Word count: 80–120 (count them — verify before finalising)
- [ ] Starts with exact job title from JD (or close synonym)
- [ ] Contains at least 1 quantified achievement
- [ ] Contains top 3–4 JD keywords in first 50 words
- [ ] Tone matches company culture (from company-intel brief)
- [ ] Zero banned phrases
- [ ] Every claim is supported by the candidate's actual experience
- [ ] Reads like a human wrote it, not a template filled in
