# Bullet Point Writing

## Critical Context
All bullets must be grounded in the candidate's **real experience**. Quantify what they actually did — never fabricate metrics. When exact numbers are unavailable, use verified proxies: ranges, frequencies, scope statements, or comparative before/after metrics.

---

## The Formula

```
[Action verb] + [what + how] + [quantified result] + [scope / timeframe]
```

Not every element is required in every bullet — but every bullet needs at least **an action** and a **measurable result or scope**. A bullet with no number and no scale is a weak bullet.

---

## 5 Bullet Structures

AI-generated text is flagged when every bullet follows the same grammatical pattern. Use at least 3 different structures across each role.

**Structure A — Action → Result (standard, most common):**
> Elicited and documented 40+ functional requirements across 6 business units, reducing development rework by 35% over two sprint cycles

**Structure B — Context → Action → Outcome:**
> During a CRM migration affecting 10,000+ users, mapped 25 data fields between legacy and target systems, achieving zero data loss at go-live

**Structure C — Scope-first → Action → Impact:**
> Across 4 cross-functional teams, coordinated UAT for a payments module, identifying and resolving 18 critical defects — zero post-launch P1 incidents

**Structure D — Challenge → Method → Result:**
> Faced with a 3-week sign-off bottleneck, redesigned the approval workflow using BPMN process mapping, cutting cycle time to 5 days

**Structure E — Metric-led → Action:**
> Reduced invoice processing time from 11 days to 4 days by redesigning the onboarding workflow across 3 departments using BPMN

Mix these across each role. Never use the same structure for consecutive bullets.

## Quantification When Exact Numbers Aren't Available

| Situation | Acceptable proxy |
|---|---|
| Rough percentage known | "reduced processing time by ~30%" |
| Exact % unknown but scale known | "across 5 cross-functional teams" |
| Volume unclear | "15+ stakeholder workshops per quarter" |
| System scale | "requirements for a platform serving 10,000+ users" |
| Before/after known | "cut sign-off cycle from 3 weeks to 5 days" |
| Revenue/cost unknown | "project valued at €2M+" |
| Team size approximate | "within a team of 8 across 3 time zones" |

**If none of the above applies:** Ask the candidate for one specific number rather than leaving the bullet unquantified. "Can you give me an approximate scale — how many users, records, or team members were involved?"

## JD Language Mirroring

The single highest-impact technique. Echo the JD's exact responsibility phrasing in the bullets — the recruiter's brain pattern-matches their own words.

**JD says:** "Collaborate with cross-functional teams to gather and document business requirements"
**Bullet writes:** "Collaborated with cross-functional teams spanning product, engineering, QA, and DevOps to gather and document 40+ business requirements, reducing rework by 35%"

**JD says:** "Develop and maintain dashboards and reports for senior stakeholders"
**Bullet writes:** "Developed and maintained 10+ Power BI dashboards for senior stakeholders, tracking billing accuracy and service lifecycle KPIs — reduced C-level reporting delays by 35%"

This works because recruiters subconsciously score resumes against the JD. When they see their own language reflected back with evidence, it registers as a perfect fit.

---

## Bullet Ordering Within a Role

Order by relevance to the target JD — not by chronological order of execution:
1. **First bullet:** maps to the JD's #1 stated responsibility
2. **Second bullet:** maps to JD's #2 responsibility
3. **Remaining bullets:** cover other JD requirements, then add breadth

## Seniority Calibration

| Level | Bullet emphasis | Language signals |
|---|---|---|
| Junior / Graduate | Learning, contribution, scope of involvement | "Contributed to," "Assisted in leading," "Developed under guidance" |
| Mid-level | Independent ownership, delivery, measurable impact | "Led," "Delivered," "Managed," "Coordinated" |
| Senior | Cross-team influence, strategic decisions, team capability | "Defined," "Championed," "Directed," "Advised stakeholders" |
| Lead / Principal | Org-level outcomes, hiring, P&L, product direction | "Built," "Scaled," "Established," "Oversaw," "Grew from X to Y" |

Don't use executive-level phrasing for a junior candidate. ATS calibration aside, experienced recruiters immediately spot the mismatch.

---

## Hard Rules

- 4–6 bullets per role (5 ideal for recent roles)
- Max 2 lines per bullet
- Never repeat the same verb twice in one role
- Never start with: "Responsible for," "Helped," "Assisted," "Worked on"
- "Supported" is acceptable **only** when ownership context immediately follows
- Every bullet has a quantified result or measurable scope
- No two consecutive bullets use the same grammatical structure
- Vary sentence length — mix punchy 1-line bullets with richer 2-line ones
- Past tense for past roles, present tense for current role

## Common Weak Bullets and Their Fixes

| Weak | Fix |
|---|---|
| "Responsible for requirements gathering" | "Gathered and documented 40+ requirements across 6 business units, reducing scope creep by 25%" |
| "Helped with data analysis" | "Analysed 500K+ transaction records using SQL, identifying 3 billing discrepancies that recovered €180K in revenue" |
| "Worked on stakeholder communication" | "Facilitated 15+ weekly stakeholder workshops across product, legal, and operations, reducing sign-off delays by 40%" |
| "Assisted the team with UAT" | "Coordinated UAT across 4 teams, identifying and resolving 18 critical defects before launch — zero post-release P1 incidents" |
| "Improved processes" | "Redesigned invoice approval workflow using BPMN, reducing cycle time from 11 days to 4 days across 3 departments" |
