# Resume & Hiring Standards 2026

## The 2026 Hiring Landscape

### How Companies Actually Hire in 2026
- **Skills-based hiring is dominant.** 81% of employers now prioritise demonstrated skills over degrees. Job titles matter less than what you can prove you've done.
- **AI is on both sides.** Companies use AI to screen; candidates use AI to write. The result: recruiters are now trained to detect AI-generated content, and ATS systems flag it. Authenticity is the new competitive advantage.
- **The hidden job market is bigger than ever.** 70–80% of roles are filled through networking, referrals, and internal moves before they're ever posted publicly. Cold applications have a 2–5% interview rate. Referred candidates: 20–50%.
- **Speed matters.** Top candidates are off the market in 10 days. The best roles get 250+ applications in the first 48 hours. Your resume has to work on first contact.

### ATS Reality (2026)
- 98% of Fortune 500 and 75% of mid-market companies use AI-powered ATS
- Modern ATS (Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, iCIMS, Ashby) uses NLP + ML:
  - Ranks by **evidence density** — keywords backed by quantified bullets score higher
  - Detects **keyword stuffing** — repeated terms without context are penalised
  - Infers **seniority** from consistency across titles, scope of responsibilities, and team size
  - Parses **skills taxonomies** — maps your skills to their internal skill graph
  - Flags **formatting errors** — tables, columns, text boxes, headers/footers cause parse failures
- 75% of resumes rejected before a human sees them — mostly formatting + keyword mismatch

### Human Review Reality (2026)
- 6–10 second first scan — the top third of page 1 is everything
- 62% of employers reject resumes that feel generic or AI-written
- Recruiters look for: specific numbers, company-relevant experience, clear progression
- 68% prefer two-page resumes for candidates with 3+ years experience
- 92% check LinkedIn before calling — resume and LinkedIn must tell the same story
- Hiring managers care about: "Can this person solve MY problem?" — not generic competence

### AI Detection Signals (What Gets You Rejected)
- Generic superlatives: "exceptional," "outstanding," "unparalleled"
- Buzzword clusters without evidence: "results-driven team player with proven track record"
- Unnaturally perfect grammar with no personality
- Repetitive sentence structures (every bullet follows the exact same pattern)
- Vague scope: "managed stakeholders" (which ones? how many? what outcome?)
- Over-polished language that doesn't match the candidate's experience level

### What Actually Gets Interviews in 2026
1. **Specificity** — "Reduced invoice processing time from 11 days to 4 days across 3 departments" beats "Improved operational efficiency"
2. **Evidence density** — Every skill claim backed by a bullet with numbers
3. **Company alignment** — Resume reads like it was written FOR this company, not AT this company
4. **Strategic networking** — A warm intro or referral alongside the application
5. **Complete package** — Resume + tailored cover letter + LinkedIn alignment

## Dominant Resume Format: Skills-First

```
1. Header (name, target role title, contact info)
2. Professional Summary (80–120 words)
3. Skills (categorised, immediately after summary)
4. Work Experience (reverse chronological, evidence-based bullets)
5. Education
6. Certifications (if relevant)
```

## ATS Formatting Rules (Non-Negotiable)
- File format: `.docx` for ATS portal submissions (97% parsing accuracy); `.pdf` for direct email to recruiters
- Generate BOTH formats for every application — the application strategy notes which to use where
- Single-column layout only
- Fonts: Calibri (primary), Arial, Times New Roman, Georgia, Verdana ONLY
- Font size: 10pt body, 16pt name, 11pt designation, 9.5pt contact, 10.5pt section headers
- Justified text (body, bullets, skills). Centred for name, designation, and contact line
- Section headers: bold, navy blue (#1F3A5F), left-aligned, blue-grey (#7F9BBF) underline border
- Company/institution lines: italic (not bold), rendered via #### markdown
- Skills lines: bold category + tab + values, hanging indent (3600 twips)
- Bullets: 340 twips indent, first bullet 12pt before, rest 1pt before. `•` or `-` only (proper Word numbering)
- Line spacing: 1.15x (276 twips, auto rule) on all paragraphs
- No empty paragraphs — all spacing via before/after only
- Margins: 12.7mm all sides (Word "Narrow" preset)
- No tables, columns, text boxes, images, logos, graphics
- Text colours: dark grey (#333333) body, navy blue (#1F3A5F) name and section headers, near-black (#2D2D2D) role titles. No coloured backgrounds
- No special characters (★, ♦, ►)
- No ghost characters (zero-width spaces, BOM, soft hyphens, smart quotes, en/em dashes)
- Contact info in main body — never in header/footer
- Dates: Month YYYY format with ASCII hyphens consistently
- Section header terms: PROFESSIONAL SUMMARY, SKILLS, WORK EXPERIENCE, EDUCATION, CERTIFICATIONS
- Metadata: author must be candidate name, no tool fingerprints

## Keyword Strategy
- Primary keywords (job title, core skills): 3–4× naturally across the document
- Secondary keywords (preferred skills, culture terms): 2–3×
- Never exceed 5× for any single keyword (triggers spam detection)
- Use exact phrasing from the job description — not synonyms
- Every keyword must be backed by evidence (a bullet that proves it)
- Acronyms: spell out on first use, e.g., "Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN)"

## Cover Letter Standards 2026
- ~60% of HR professionals read cover letters; 83% say a well-written one can secure an interview even with a weaker CV
- 21% read the cover letter BEFORE the resume; 40% read it after the resume
- 81% of hiring managers say tailored applications are "important" or "very important"
- Tailored cover letters have a 53% higher callback rate than no cover letter, and 31% higher than generic ones
- 88% of hiring managers say they can tell when AI wrote a cover letter — authenticity is critical
- 250–400 words maximum
- Structure: specific company hook → 2–3 quantified achievements → confident close
- Generic openers are worse than no cover letter
- The hook must reference something specific about the company that you couldn't copy-paste to another application

## Anti-Patterns (Instant Rejection Signals)
- "Passionate professional with a proven track record..."
- "Results-driven," "hardworking," "team player" without proof
- Keyword stuffing without evidence
- One-size-fits-all content not tailored to the role
- Vague bullets: "responsible for managing stakeholders"
- AI-sounding language patterns
- Inconsistent dates or formatting
- Skills listed that aren't backed by any experience bullet
